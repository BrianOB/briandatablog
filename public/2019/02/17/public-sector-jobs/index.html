Public sector jobs in Pittsburgh and other metros

2019-02-17

How does Pittsburgh’s public sector employment compare to that of other metro areas? In this first glance, I’m going to focus on what percentage of the metro area’s jobs are government jobs.

The data comes from the Bureau of Labor Statistics monthly survey of state and local employment. I use the annual averages, so the latest data is for 2017.

Looking at 444 metro areas in the 50 states, the District of Columbia and Puerto Rico, the concentration of public-sector employment in 2017 ranged from a low of 6.0% of all jobs in the metro area to a high of 42.0%.

Pittsburgh was on the low end of the range with about 10.0% of its jobs in some branch of government.

You might think Washington, D.C., metro area would have one of highest concentrations but only 21.0% of its jobs were in the public sector in 2017.

The metros with the five highest concentrations were:

Ames, IA 41.0%
